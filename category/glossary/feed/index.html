en in the industry for the past thirty-odd years. That doesn’t have to be true! Here’s a short dictionary of cryptocurrency terms that you’ll read when it comes to bitcoin…

  • ASIC:

An abbreviation for Application Specific Integrated Circuit.

  • Bitcoin address:

A bitcoin address is a strong of letters and numbers identifying a bitcoin transaction. Each individual transaction has its own bitcoin address. This should be kept safe, as long your bitcoin address means losing access to the transaction!

  • Bitcoin:

The term “bitcoin” was first coined by Satoshi Nakamoto in 2008 to describe a new kind of digitally encrypted currency that could be exchanged between people. It is often abbreviated to BTC.

  • Bitcoin mining:

Bitcoin mining is linking up to the public bitcoin ledger to verify existing transactions and calculate new ones to the system.

  • Bitcoin miner:

A device that enables users to mine bitcoins. A person mining bitcoins is also known as a miner.

  • Bitcoin wallet:

A bitcoin wallet is just like your old-fashioned kind of wallet, except it exists in a software or hardware form and is used to securely store your bitcoins.

  • Blockchain:

A blockchain is a ledger that records transactions as they happen in the form of a “chain”, ensuring that the transaction remains safe and cannot be altered or hacked.

  • BTC:

The abbreviation for bitcoins.

  • Cryptocurrency:

A cryptocurrency is a currency that exists and changes in cyberspace, of which bitcoin is one.

Decentralized:

Bitcoin is a decentralized currency – this means that it is not controlled or affected by a central financial agency, but instead dependent on the millions of transactions part of the public ledger.

  • Finney, Hal:

The first bitcoin transaction ever took place between creator Satoshi Nakamoto and Hal Finney when Finney was sent 10 bitcoins as a test.

  • Genesis Block, The:

The first-ever block to be mined by the creator of bitcoin is known as the Genesis Block. It is also the term used for the first block of a general blockchain.

Hardware wallet:

A hardware wallet is a physical bitcoin wallet. This can be in the form of a card or flash drive.

  • Millibitcoin:

A sub-denomination of bitcoin that represents one thousandth of a bitcoin. It is abbreviated to mBTC.

  • mBTC:

An abbreviation for milibitcoin.

  • Satoshi:

The smallest denomination of bitcoin, one millionth of a bitcoin. It is abbreviated to sat.

  • Sat:

An abbreviation for satoshi.

  • Satoshi client:

The first bitcoin software to be released is also named after the creator of bitcoin and is known as the Satoshi client.

  • Satoshi, Nakamoto:

The creator of bitcoin, known only as Satoshi Nakamoto.  

  • Software wallet:

A software version of your bitcoin wallet, hosted on a computer, smartphone or online.
