Bitcoin in the Headlines

You are here:
  1. Home
  2. Bitcoin
  3. Bitcoin in the Headlines

Have you been keeping an eye on the news? Bitcoins and cryptocurrency has been making news the world over, and we’ve collected some of the recent headlines about bitcoin in world news just in case there is anything important you might have missed.

Click on the story’s headline to be redirected to the original hosted on each publication’s website.

  • IOL: Pick n Pay Accepts Bitcoin as a Payment Option During Trial Run

18 September, 2017

South African retailer Pick n Pay dipped their toes into the water in September 2017 and trialed the potential of accepting bitcoin payments from customers. This comes shortly after their announcement of Pick n Pay Store Credit, and we tip our hats to them for keeping an eye out for the South African consumer and make it both easier and safer to pay for their groceries.

  • Business Live: “No Paying via Bitcoin Just Yet”, says Pick n Pay

19 September, 2017

After many enquiries, Pick n Pay had to clarify that it had been a trial run to test the potential of bitcoin in-store, and that they were not accepting bitcoins as mainstream payments just yet. But, as a positive note, they acknowledged that it could work as an option in future pending regulations.

  • Businesstech: How Bitcoin Could be Regulated in South Africa

21 August, 2017

The South African Reserve Bank themselves have spotted the potential in bitcoin investment and suggested ways that bitcoin could be controlled, regulated and exchanged in South Africa – and where even more investment opportunities could lie for South African consumers in future.

  • MyBroadband: How Much South Africans Have Invested in Bitcoin

1 October, 2017

MyBroadband launched their Cryptocurrency Survey amongst 1, 598 users and was happy to find that, of the people asked, an astonishing 78.86% of them said that they would like to invest more in bitcoins and other cryptocurrencies in future.

  • BusinessTech: Thousands of Bitcoin Being Traded in South Africa Every Week

14 November 2017

We like good news, and the head of marketing for cryptocurrency exchange at Luno announced in November 2017 that thousands of bitcoins were being traded in South Africa every week.

  • Fin24: Bitcoin Surges in Zim After Military Moves to Seize Power

11 November 2017

Fin24 reported on the effect the recent political changes in Zimbabwe had on the prices of bitcoin – not too surprisingly, people have been eager to find out more and invest, joining the rest of the world.

  • MyBroadband: Bitcoin Rockets Through R150, 000

26 November 2017

Cryptocurency-watch Lumo announced that bitcoin prices have reached a record high.

  • Bonus Headline: The Big Bang Theory

If you’re streaming your TV shows through Netflix, catch the next episode of The Big Bang Theory Season 11. Bitcoins will be a huge part of the episode, and we can only wonder… See a sneak peak of the episode on YouTube from TV Promos here.
